Before approaching homeless puppies, ensure their safety and assess the environment. Observe from a distance to check if the puppies are truly abandoned or if their mother is nearby. Look for any obvious signs of injury, illness, or distress. If the puppies seem in immediate danger (such as being on a busy street), it's important to act quickly. However, if their mother is around, it may be best to leave the puppies alone, as she is likely taking care of them. Only intervene if it's absolutely necessary to ensure their safety.

Once you’ve confirmed the puppies are abandoned or in danger, carefully secure them. If possible, wear gloves to minimize stress and scent transfer, especially if you’re unsure whether they’re wild or have been socialized. Gently place the puppies in a safe container, like a crate or box, to transport them. Ensure the container is warm and secure, as puppies are sensitive to temperature and can easily become stressed. Keep them calm during transport by minimizing noise and sudden movements. If the puppies are very young, they may need immediate attention, such as bottle-feeding or heat sources.

After safely rescuing the puppies, the next priority is their health. Take them to a veterinarian for a thorough checkup, vaccinations, and deworming. The vet can also provide guidance on their age, nutrition, and any special care they might need. If you're not able to keep the puppies yourself, reach out to local animal rescues, shelters, or foster networks for assistance. It's crucial to help them find a safe, loving home where they can receive the care and attention they need. If you're fostering, provide them with the proper socialization and training until they’re ready for adoption.
